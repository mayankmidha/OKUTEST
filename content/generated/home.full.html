Dr. Suraj Singh

I’m Dr. Suraj Singh (he/him), a Psychiatrist with over __ years of experience in mental health care. My approach is grounded in the belief that emotional distress is rarely just about isolated symptoms — it often reflects deeper psychological patterns, life experiences, and social realities. I aim to understand each person in the full context of their inner and outer world, not just through a diagnostic lens.

I offer care that is thoughtful, collaborative, and personalised. Whether someone is navigating anxiety, depression, PTSD, ADHD, substance use concerns, or gender identity-related challenges, I bring sensitivity and clinical clarity to our work together. I’ve had the privilege of supporting many individuals from LGBTQIA+ communities, and I understand how important it is for psychiatric care to feel safe, affirming, and respectful of lived experience.

In our sessions, I take time to listen — not just to what is said, but to what might be difficult to express. I value transparency in diagnosis and treatment, and I work with patients to co-create a plan that makes sense to them — one that integrates both medication and psychotherapy as active, complementary parts of the healing process.

I pay close attention to psychosocial stressors, identity, relationships, and early life influences, while also staying grounded in evidence-based psychiatric care. My aim is not only to reduce suffering, but to support a more coherent, stable, and empowered sense of self.

All sessions are held online, offering accessible, confidential support from the comfort of your chosen space.

Tanisha Singh

Some wounds don’t always show up as symptoms at first. They appear in the way we relate, in how we retreat, in what we chase and what we silence. My work begins in those quiet spaces—where pain doesn’t always announce itself, but still shapes everything.

I’m a clinical psychologist with a background in psychodynamic psychotherapy. My approach in treatment integrates therapeutic modalities depending on the needs of the client. Moreover my understanding of the work is also supplemented by my own lived experiences, which allows me a deeper understanding and sensitivity towards certain ways pain shows up for us as individuals. I’m also someone who lives with a chronic illness and the effects of trauma. Someone who perhaps knows how it feels to carry invisible weight. Someone who believes that healing is not a solo pursuit.

My understanding of the work is depth-oriented, relational, and grounded in object relations theory. But it’s also tender, curious, and influenced by certain humanistic schools of thought—narrative practice, existential frameworks, and the power of a shared story. All in all, I prefer to tailor my approach to the individual in front of me and their specific needs at that moment.

During a session, my focus is not simply on the supportive function of therapy, but also to help explore insights about oneself, their history as well as their present relational world. Moreover, I also pay close attention to how trauma may be operating within the lived experiences of the client and sometimes also draw on somatic awareness, movement, and body-based attunement to support the psyche’s deeper processes.

Clients often come to me after a long time of feeling stuck or misunderstood, or drained and feeling a deep ache. For example, those navigating a difficult childhood, trauma, grief, despair, chronic health concerns, or a lingering sense of meaninglessness. Or those whose childhoods felt too lonely, too chaotic, too parentified. Those who learned to survive too early—but now long to live with depth. Ultimately, the all-encompassing understanding is that all healing is dependent on the therapeutic relationship between the client and the therapist.

I don’t see therapy as a place to fix or optimize the self. I see it as a space to slow down. I believe therapy is not always about becoming more. Sometimes it’s about becoming real. And from there, slowly building a life that feels lived—not just managed.

To understand what shaped you. To feel what couldn’t be felt before. And to begin, sometimes for the first time.

Amna Ansari

At the heart of my work is a deep belief in the power of human connection and the transformative nature of self-understanding. As a licensed Clinical Psychologist with over three years of experience, I provide thoughtful, evidence-based therapy grounded in both clinical expertise and a deep respect for each person’s unique story.

Therapy, at its core, is a relationship—a space where you can be seen, heard, and understood without judgment. I bring both professional training and a warm, grounded presence to the therapeutic process. My work is informed by evidence-based practices such as Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT), Dialectical Behaviour Therapy (DBT), and mindfulness-based interventions—but it’s guided, first and foremost, by you.

I work with children, adolescents, and adults navigating a broad spectrum of emotional and psychological challenges, including anxiety, depression, mood disorders, and intellectual or developmental difficulties. While clinical structure provides a foundation, I tailor each therapeutic journey to the person sitting across from me—taking into account their inner world, their strengths, and the context of their life.

My background also includes two years as a research psychologist exploring Emotional Intelligence, a lens that continues to shape my therapeutic approach. I believe that developing a deeper awareness of our emotional world is central to healing and meaningful change.

My intention is to create a safe, steady space where you can begin to untangle what feels stuck, discover more adaptive ways of coping, and reconnect with your sense of agency. I see therapy as a collaborative process—one that values authenticity, trust, and the courage it takes to do this kind of inner work.

Whether you’re struggling with something specific or simply feeling overwhelmed by life’s complexity, I’m here to support you in moving toward greater clarity, balance, emotional resilience and fulfilling lives.

Mohit Dudeja

As a queer affirmative psychotherapist, my practice is grounded in a deep respect for the complexities of identity, relational life, and emotional experience. Drawing from a psychodynamic framework, I offer a space where unconscious patterns, formative attachments, and embodied memories can be gently explored over time. I believe that therapy is not about arriving at quick answers, but about cultivating a space where your inner life can be witnessed, held, and slowly understood in all its depth and nuance.

My practice centers those who are seeking not just relief, but insight—people who are curious about their emotional worlds and are open to engaging in sustained, reflective work. I work with individuals navigating a range of experiences, including those shaped by queerness, trauma, and the often invisible burdens of marginalization. These are not merely “issues” to be solved; they are lived realities that deserve tenderness, complexity, and analytic attention.

I understand psychotherapy as a co-created process—one in which we trace the threads of past and present, and where silence, emotion, and language each carry meaning. The therapeutic relationship itself becomes a vital site of transformation: a place to explore how you have been seen, misrecognized, or silenced in other relationships—and to reimagine what it means to be fully known.

My work is informed not only by psychological theories but by my own lived experience as a queer person of color. I approach each person with a commitment to honoring the fullness of who they are—not only in their struggles, but in their resilience, contradictions, and capacity for growth.

It is through this careful attention to what is often unspoken that a more integrated and expansive sense of self can begin to take shape.

Gursheel Kaur

Hello,

I am Gursheel Kaur (she/her), a psychotherapist in private practice. My practice is grounded in the psychodynamic framework, modeled as per the specific needs of the patients with an emphasis on relational perspective. I am currently practicing under supervision.

The imagination of the practice that I carry is one of a holding space, that does not resort to hasty solutions. The “here and now” is pivotal to the process with an emphasis on staying with one’s experience in all its silences and organic verbalizations. While initial sessions may focus on specific goals, future sessions are not limited to them. Instead, individuals are encouraged to openly explore early life experiences and meaningful events that may have influenced their current sense of self. This approach to psychotherapy does not rely on diagnosing disorders, as it may limit possibilities in therapy.
My personal engagement involves use of visualisations and creative metaphors weaving a constellation of playfulness into therapy. Through such relational work I attempt to witness a person’s subjective life-world, guided by a flow of curiosity. I firmly believe that loving and suffering, rupture and repair are not too far apart.
